The Scenario
Shopify helps millions of merchants sell online. One of the most important steps in setting up a store is configuring shipping rates — the rules that determine what customers pay for delivery.
Here's the problem: 40% of merchants who start setting up shipping rates abandon the flow before completing it. They begin the setup, get partway through, and leave without finishing. This means their stores either can't ship orders or default to settings that may not match their business.

Insight
The research suggests that merchants are not abandoning shipping setup because they do not care about it, but because the process feels high-risk, complex, and unfamiliar. Many new merchants lack logistics knowledge and are overwhelmed by decisions such as shipping zones, carrier rates, free shipping thresholds, and international shipping rules. At the same time, uncertainty around industry standards and fear of costly mistakes, like undercharging for shipping or losing money,  creates hesitation and procrastination. Overall, the core issue appears to be a lack of confidence rather than a lack of ability, indicating an opportunity for Shopify to provide more guidance, recommendations, and simplified setup experiences.
